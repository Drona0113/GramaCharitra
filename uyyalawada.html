Why "Uyyalawada"?

"Uyyala" (ఉయ్యాల)

Means "swing" in Telugu, often symbolizing balance, rhythm, and nurturing.

"Wada" (వాడ)

A suffix used for settlements or hamlets, especially ones established by feudal lineages.

Symbolic Interpretation

"Settlement of the Uyyala family" or "Swing of power" representing its revolutionary past.

Historical Timeline

Early Origins (Pre-Colonial Era)

Feudal hamlet home to palegarlu (local chieftains) managing agricultural lands and irrigation systems.

The Rise of Narasimha Reddy (1806-1847)

Born to a zamindari family, trained in warfare and angered by British oppressive policies.

The 1846-47 Revolt

Led a rebellion of 5,000+ fighters against British rule, years before the 1857 Revolt.

Martyrdom (Feb 22, 1847)

Captured due to betrayal and publicly executed. His head displayed for 30 years as warning.

Post-Independence Recognition

Honored as "First Freedom Fighter of India" with memorials, Jayanti celebrations, and curriculum inclusion.

Village Life

Kundu River Basin

Located in the Rayalaseema dry plains with red loamy and black regur soils.

Agriculture

Tank-based irrigation supports crops like groundnut, bajra, cotton, and sunflower.

Rural Economy

Small cattle herding, goat rearing, pottery and hand-weaving supplement agriculture.

Cultural Heritage

Veerabhadra Swamy Temple

Guardian deity of warriors worshipped especially during Uyyalawada Utsavams.

Uyyalawada Jayanti

Annual celebration on Feb 22 with flag marches, plays, and freedom pledge ceremonies.

Cultural Performances

Burra Katha, Veeranatyam, and Paata katha performances keep the revolutionary spirit alive.

Key Attractions

Narasimha Reddy Statue

Heroic statue depicting the freedom fighter mounted on horseback, sword raised.

Revolutionary Trail

Sites connected to the 1846-47 rebellion including hideouts and battle locations.

Local Economy

Dryland Farming

Major crops include groundnut, bajra (pearl millet), cotton, sunflower, and red gram.

Livestock Rearing

Small cattle herding and goat rearing supplement agricultural income.

Handicrafts

Seasonal pottery and hand-weaving provide additional livelihood options.

Fascinating Facts

First Freedom Fighter

Narasimha Reddy's rebellion predated the 1857 Revolt by a decade.

Preserved Artifacts

His sword and armor are preserved in a private zamindar family museum.

Cultural Legacy

Mentioned in freedom struggle ballads sung during Telangana and Andhra protests.

Airport Naming

Kurnool Airport was renamed Uyyalawada Narasimha Reddy Airport in 2021.

Brutal Aftermath

His head was displayed on a pole for 30 years as a British warning.

Pop Culture

Featured in the 2019 biopic "Sye Raa Narasimha Reddy" starring Chiranjeevi.

Why "Annavaram"?

"Anna" (అన్న)

Means "food" in Telugu, symbolizing life, sustenance, and prosperity. Represents the temple's Annadanam tradition.

"Varam" (వరం)

Means "boon" or "gift" in Telugu. Signifies the divine blessings granted by Satyanarayana Swamy.

Sacred Interpretation

"The place where food is given as a boon" or "The blessed village of sustenance" reflecting the temple's traditions.

Historical Timeline

Mythological Era

Lord Satyanarayana Swamy manifested on Ratnagiri Hill. Sage Rishyashringa performed yagnas here.

Ancient Period

Small shrine existed before 12th century. Eastern Chalukyas and Reddy rulers patronized the area.

Modern Revival (1891)

Current temple constructed by Sri Prakasa Rao Panthulu after discovering the original deity.

Early 1900s

Temple gained popularity across Andhra. Satyanarayana Vratams were institutionalized.

Post-Independence

Became one of the richest temples in AP. Added ghat roads, complexes, and mass Annadanam centers.

Natural Treasures

Ratnagiri Hill

Sacred hill resembling a conch shell when viewed from distance. Offers panoramic views of Pampa River.

Lush Surroundings

Paddy fields, palm groves, and mango orchards surround the hill, creating a serene environment.

Sacred Sites

Satyanarayana Swamy Temple

Main shrine with unique Trimurti idol combining Vishnu's chakra, Shiva's trishula, and Brahma's kamandalu.

Annadanam Complex

Where thousands are fed daily as part of the temple's sacred food offering tradition.

Pravachana Mandapam

Foothill venue hosting year-round spiritual discourses and religious teachings.

Major Festivals

Satyanarayana Vrata

Daily and mass vratas performed with prasadam distribution and chanting circles.

Karthika Masam

Month-long celebrations with lamp lighting, bhajans, and deepa offerings.

Brahmotsavams

Annual festival with grand processions in various vahanams and cultural programs.

Local Economy

Temple Services

Supports thousands through prasadam production, lodging, and temple shops.

Local Products

Panchamritam bottles, temple turmeric, vermilion, and sacred rice packets.

Agriculture

Paddy, coconuts, and mangoes cultivated in surrounding fertile lands.

Little-Known Facts

Unique Trimurti Idol

One of few temples where Vishnu is worshipped alongside Shiva and Brahma elements.

10,000+ Vratas Monthly

More than 10,000 Satyanarayana Vratas are performed each month by devotees.

Healing Prasadam

The temple prasadam is considered a cure for stress and negativity by devotees.

Spiritual Discourses

Pravachana Mandapam hosts year-round spiritual teachings at the foothill.

Mass Annadanam

Thousands are fed daily through the temple's sacred food offering tradition.

Conch-Shaped Hill

Ratnagiri Hill resembles a shankha (conch) when viewed from a distance.

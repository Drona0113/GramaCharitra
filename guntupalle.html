Why "Guntupalle"?

"Guntu" (గుంటు)

Telugu word meaning "pit", "hole", or "cave", referencing the village's ancient rock-cut Buddhist caves.

"Palle" (పల్లె)

Means "village" or "hamlet". Together, "Guntupalle" translates to "Village of Caves".

Historical Accuracy

The name reflects the ancient Buddhist cave complexes carved into the hillside around 1st century BCE.

Historical Timeline

3rd Century BCE

Peak of Buddhist activity with construction of viharas, chaityas, and rock-cut stupas.

Post-Buddhist Era

Continued as a pilgrimage site influenced by Mahayana and later Hindu traditions.

British Era

Documented by archaeologists like Alexander Rea in late 19th century.

Modern Times

Maintained by Archaeological Survey of India with growing tourism and conservation.

Buddhist Monuments

Maha Stupa

Circular brick-and-stone stupa, one of India's oldest Buddhist structures.

Chaityagrihas

Semi-circular cave halls with meditation rooms oriented toward sunrise.

Viharas

Monk living quarters carved into the hillside with stone railings.

Modern Sacred Sites

Poleramma Gudi

Village guardian goddess worshipped for protection and prosperity.

Subramanya Swamy

Temple associated with valor, popular among youth and farmers.

Naga Shrines

Sacred serpent worship sites continuing from Buddhist naga symbolism.

Festivals & Culture

Buddha Jayanti

Celebrated with monks from Visakhapatnam and Amaravati at the ancient caves.

Sankranti

Traditional rangoli and bull races during this harvest festival.

Burra Katha

Storytelling performances on Buddhist legends and local history.

Village Economy

Agriculture

Paddy, chillies, sunflower, jowar & pulses cultivation using traditional methods.

Tourism

ASI-trained guides lead heritage tours of the Buddhist caves.

Handicrafts

Local artisans create Buddha statues and traditional pottery for sale.

Little-Known Facts

Older Than Ajanta

Guntupalle's stupa predates many caves in Maharashtra.

Secret Tunnel

Hidden passage behind a chaitya believed to be a monk escape route.

Dharma Chakra

Ancient carved wheel with Brahmi script still visible on main hill.

Cool Caves

The rock-cut caves maintain a cool temperature year-round.

Serpent Worship

Continues from Buddhist naga symbolism to modern times.

Heritage Education

Buddhist Heritage Museum Learning Center educates visitors.
